# Content Density and Citation QA Checklist

## Slide Occupancy

- [ ] Ordinary content slides use approximately 70–88% of the main content area.
- [ ] Slides below approximately 60% occupancy were enriched or merged.
- [ ] Slides above approximately 90% occupancy were split or simplified.
- [ ] No large unintentional empty half-slide remains.
- [ ] No single blank region exceeds approximately 18% of the slide unless intentional.

## Information Balance

- [ ] Each substantive slide contains at least two meaningful content units.
- [ ] Content is distributed across columns rather than concentrated in one corner.
- [ ] Top, middle, and bottom zones are balanced.
- [ ] Evidence and interpretation have appropriate relative visual weight.
- [ ] Decorative images are not used to mask missing content.

## Text and Readability

- [ ] Body text is at least 16 pt.
- [ ] References are at least 9 pt and readable after rendering.
- [ ] A sparse page was not fixed only by making the title or image oversized.
- [ ] An overcrowded page was not fixed by shrinking text excessively.

## Citation Coverage

- [ ] Every substantive slide has a source footer.
- [ ] Introduction slides contain 2–4 sources when multiple claims are made.
- [ ] Theory slides cite the original theory and later application/review/critique.
- [ ] Method slides cite original methods papers or official documentation.
- [ ] Result slides cite the target paper and figure/table number where relevant.
- [ ] Discussion slides cite comparison studies and mechanism evidence.
- [ ] Appraisal slides cite reporting or methodological standards where relevant.
- [ ] Implication slides cite the paper and any external methodological recommendation.

## Final Render Review

- [ ] Every slide was rendered and visually inspected.
- [ ] Sparse and imbalanced slides were identified from the rendered deck, not only from the editable file.
- [ ] Reference footers remain visible in presentation mode.
