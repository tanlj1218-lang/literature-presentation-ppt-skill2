# Academic QA Checklist

## Bibliographic Information

- [ ] Title matches the article.
- [ ] Journal name is correct.
- [ ] Publication and online dates are distinguished.
- [ ] DOI is correct.
- [ ] First and corresponding authors are correct.
- [ ] Affiliations are correct.
- [ ] Funding and registration are correct.
- [ ] Impact factor includes year and source.
- [ ] JCR quartile includes year.
- [ ] CAS partition includes version/year or is marked unverified.

## Scientific Narrative

- [ ] The introduction follows one coherent line.
- [ ] The gap is specific rather than generic.
- [ ] The study design logically answers the research question.
- [ ] Research aims/hypotheses are accurately stated.

## Methods

- [ ] Design and setting are accurate.
- [ ] Recruitment and sample are accurate.
- [ ] Inclusion and exclusion criteria are separated.
- [ ] Sample-size rationale is reported accurately.
- [ ] Data collection timing is accurate.
- [ ] Quality-control steps are not invented.
- [ ] Missing-data handling is reported.
- [ ] Instruments and scoring are verified.
- [ ] Covariates and variable roles are accurate.
- [ ] Statistical methods and parameters are accurate.
- [ ] External methodological recommendations are not presented as article facts.

## Results

- [ ] Participant numbers match all relevant sections.
- [ ] Baseline values match the original table.
- [ ] Effect sizes, confidence intervals, and P values are correct.
- [ ] Figures and tables are linked to the correct source.
- [ ] Statistical significance is not confused with clinical importance.
- [ ] Association is not presented as causation.
- [ ] Simulation findings are not presented as validated interventions.
- [ ] Sensitivity and stability results are included when important.

## Discussion

- [ ] Main findings answer the research questions.
- [ ] Prior studies are represented accurately.
- [ ] Mechanistic explanations are supported or clearly labeled as inference.
- [ ] Author limitations and independent appraisal are distinguished.
- [ ] Generalizability is appropriately limited.

## User-Research Implications

- [ ] Suggestions are specific to the user's research.
- [ ] Transferable and non-transferable elements are separated.
- [ ] Parameter values are not copied without checking suitability.
- [ ] Potential paper ideas are methodologically plausible.

## References and Traceability

- [ ] Every major external claim has a source.
- [ ] Every key number has a traceable source location.
- [ ] Every cropped figure/table has original numbering.
- [ ] Evidence tracker is complete.
- [ ] Unresolved issues are disclosed.
