# 45-Minute Literature Presentation Outline

Use this as a starting point rather than a rigid page count.

## A. Opening and Article Profile — 3 minutes, 3–4 slides

1. Title slide.
2. Journal profile: cover, IF/year, JCR, CAS partition/version, publication date, DOI.
3. Authors, affiliations, country/region, funding, registration.
4. Optional: why this paper was selected.

## B. Abstract and Roadmap — 2 minutes, 2 slides

5. Structured abstract: background, objective, methods, results, conclusion.
6. Navigation/roadmap.

## C. Introduction — 6 minutes, 4–6 slides

7. Clinical/scientific burden.
8. Core concept and current knowledge.
9. Existing evidence and common approaches.
10. Specific limitations of prior research.
11. Research gap and study entry point.
12. Aim, questions, or hypotheses.

## D. Methods — 11 minutes, 8–12 slides

13. Study design and setting.
14. Participants and recruitment.
15. Inclusion/exclusion criteria.
16. Sample-size rationale and final sample.
17. Data-collection timeline.
18. Quality control and missing-data handling.
19–21. Instruments/measures.
22. Variables and covariates.
23–25. Statistical analysis: question → method → parameter → output.
26. Analysis workflow summary.

## E. Results — 13 minutes, 12–16 slides

27. Participant flow.
28. Sample characteristics.
29. Variable distributions or measurement characteristics.
30–39. Primary and secondary results, one major conclusion per slide.
40. Robustness/sensitivity/subgroup result.
41. Integrated answer to the research questions.

## F. Discussion and Appraisal — 6 minutes, 5–7 slides

42. Main findings mapped to research questions.
43. Comparison with prior studies.
44. Possible mechanisms or reasons.
45. Clinical/theoretical implications.
46. Strengths and author-reported limitations.
47. Independent methodological appraisal.
48. Three to five substantive questions and possible improvements.

## G. Summary — 2 minutes, 1–2 slides

49. What the study added.
50. What should be interpreted cautiously.

## H. Implications for the User's Research — 2–4 minutes, 3–5 slides

51. Transferable research question/design ideas.
52. Time points, instruments, variables, and covariates.
53. Statistical methods and parameter adaptation.
54. Writing and visualization lessons.
55. What must not be copied directly / possible paper idea.

## I. Appendix — 5–15 slides

- data structure;
- scale scoring details;
- extended methods;
- complete code;
- parameter glossary;
- additional tables/figures;
- stability and sensitivity analysis;
- evidence tracker summary;
- unresolved issues.
